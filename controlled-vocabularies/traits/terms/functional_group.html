Plankton cc

IRI https://aphia.org/ns/traits/functional_group#plankton
Description

Organisms that cannot maintain a fixed location in the water column, and are thus moved by the tides and currents.

General Non-Constitutive Mixoplankton cc

IRI https://aphia.org/ns/traits/functional_group#general_non_constitutive_mixoplankton
Description

NCMs that acquire their capacity for phototrophy from general (i.e. non-specific) phototrophic prey (cf. SNCM). Non-constitutive mixoplankton (NCMs) is protist plankton that acquire the capability for phototrophy from consumption (via phagotrophy) of phototrophic prey.

skos:inScheme https://aphia.org/ns/traits/

Phytoplankton cc

IRI https://aphia.org/ns/traits/functional_group#phytoplankton
Description

Plankton obtaining nourishment via photo(auto)trophy and osmo(hetero)trophy. They are incapable of phagotrophy; cf. mixoplankton.

skos:inScheme https://aphia.org/ns/traits/

Picoplankton cc

IRI https://aphia.org/ns/traits/functional_group#picoplankton

Benthopelagic cc

IRI https://aphia.org/ns/traits/functional_group#benthopelagic
Description

Organisms living and feeding near the bottom as well as in midwaters or near the surface. Feeding on benthic as well as free swimming organisms. Many freshwater fish are opportunistic feeders that forage on the bottom as well as in midwater and near the surface, also pertaining to forms which hover or swim just over the floor of the sea, e.g. Halosauridae, Macrouridae, Moridae, Brotulidae; the depth zone about 100 metres off the bottom at all depths below the edge of the continental shelf.
